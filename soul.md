# Tuezday Soul

Tuezday exists because modern GTM has a memory problem.

The founder knows the positioning. Sales hears the objection. Ads learn which angle works. Content finds the line people repeat back. The CRM holds fragments of truth. Then the next campaign starts like none of that happened.

That reset is the thing we are here to end.

Tuezday is building GTM that remembers: one shared brain for the company, its campaigns, its channels, its tools, and its feedback loops. Not more disconnected output. Not another tab asking, "so what do we sell again?" A system where every GTM action starts with the context the company already earned.

## The belief

GTM should compound.

Every launch, post, outbound reply, ad test, sales call, customer note, rejected draft, and campaign retro should make the next move sharper. If a company learns something useful, that learning should not die in a Slack thread, a dashboard screenshot, or the founder's head.

The best GTM teams do not just produce more. They remember better.

Tuezday is built around that belief. The brain comes before the pipeline. The context comes before the generation. The approval loop comes before automation. The learning loop comes before scale.

If the system does not know more, act better, or learn faster, it is probably noise.

## What we are building

Tuezday is the memory layer and orchestration system for founder-led SaaS GTM.

At the center is the Central Brain:

- `soul`: why the company exists, what it believes, and what it refuses to be.
- `icp`: who the company sells to, what they care about, and who it should not chase.
- `voice`: how the company sounds when it is clear, useful, and real.
- `history`: what has happened, what worked, what failed, and what the market already taught us.
- `now`: what matters this week, which campaigns are live, and what the system should prioritize.

Every module should resolve context from that same brain. Content, outbound, ads, PR, CRM actions, lifecycle, reporting: different surfaces, same memory.

The product should feel less like an AI writer and more like a GTM teammate who has actually been paying attention.

## Who we are for

We are building first for founder-led SaaS companies and small GTM teams.

They are moving fast. The product is still changing. Positioning lives in too few heads. The website is almost true. The CRM is half-useful. The founder's notes are better than the official messaging doc. AI tools help in pieces, then forget everything by the next prompt.

These teams do not need a lecture on why GTM matters. They need a system that respects the messy middle and turns it into usable context.

Tuezday should help them:

- stop re-explaining the company to every new tool, hire, agency, and AI chat;
- make campaigns feel like they came from one company, not five disconnected channels;
- carry learnings from ads into content, from sales calls into outbound, from rejected drafts into better future drafts;
- keep humans in control where trust matters;
- launch faster without sounding generic;
- make the founder's GTM brain usable by the whole system.

## What we refuse

We are not building a generic AI writer.

If Tuezday becomes "type a prompt, get content," we lost the plot.

We are not building a content scheduler with smarter captions. Scheduling is an execution detail. The real product is context, coordination, approval, and learning.

We are not building a CRM. The CRM is a source and destination. It is not the brain.

We are not building an ads manager. Ad platforms execute. Tuezday should help the company decide what to say, why it should say it, what the market taught it, and what to do next.

We are not building workflow automation with prompt glitter on top. Moving data around is useful. Knowing what the data means for the next GTM action is the actual job.

We are not hiding the brain inside opaque AI magic. The core brain should be readable. A founder should be able to open it and say, "Yes, this is what we believe. This is who we sell to. This is how we sound. This is what we learned."

## How the product should feel

Tuezday should feel like a GTM command center with a memory.

Quiet. Sharp. Useful. Founder-native. A little allergic to corporate sludge.

The user should feel:

- "Finally, the system understands us."
- "It knows what we are trying to do right now."
- "The first draft is closer because it started with real context."
- "Approving work is faster because I can see why it said what it said."
- "The last campaign actually taught the next one something."

The product should never make GTM feel like it is happening behind the user's back. It should show its work, ask for approval when trust matters, and turn decisions into reusable learning.

## Operating principles

### Brain first

The brain is the platform primitive. Do not build pipelines, scrapers, senders, or integrations before the brain can carry the company context in plain English.

### Context before output

Useful GTM output starts with usable context. Prompt cleverness cannot rescue a system that does not know what the company believes, who it sells to, what it promised, or what the market already said.

### Humans stay in control

Tuezday can draft, recommend, assemble, and learn. The human approves, edits, rejects, and teaches the system what good looks like.

### Learning has somewhere to go

Every approval, rejection, campaign result, market signal, and edit should either teach the brain or clearly be ignored. Feedback that does not change future work is just theater.

### Modules share one memory

Content, outbound, ads, lifecycle, PR, and CRM actions should not each invent a separate version of the company. They should resolve from the same brain, then adapt to their channel.

### Visible trust beats hidden cleverness

The user should be able to inspect the context before generation, see why it was included, and understand what changed after a decision.

## The standard

Tuezday is working when customers say:

- "I do not have to re-explain our company every time."
- "Our content, outbound, and ads finally sound like the same company."
- "The system remembers what worked."
- "The founder is no longer the only place the GTM brain lives."

The main metric is not volume.

It is context reuse.

More output can still be generic. More reusable context makes every channel sharper.

That is the company.

GTM should not reset every week.

Tuezday exists to make it remember.
