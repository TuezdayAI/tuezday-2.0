# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Tuezday is for founder-led SaaS companies and small GTM teams that already have multiple channels in motion: content, outbound, ads, CRM, PR, social, and campaign reporting. The primary user is a founder, operator, or GTM lead who is busy, context-rich, and tired of re-explaining the company to every tool.

They open the product to understand what GTM needs attention, move a campaign forward, approve work, inspect the shared brain, and make sure learning from one channel carries into the next.

## Product Purpose

Tuezday is the memory layer and command center for modern GTM. It gives each workspace a visible, editable GTM brain, then uses that brain across campaigns, content, outbound, paid, PR, CRM, social, reporting, and learning.

Success means the app no longer feels like a pile of route tabs. It should feel like one operating loop: Brain -> Campaigns -> Create -> Review -> Publish/Send -> Measure -> Learn -> Brain.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, structured, founder-aware.

The product should sound and feel like a capable teammate who knows the GTM mess: the seven tabs, the draft that forgot the campaign, the CRM field nobody trusts, and the ad angle that should have informed outbound. It is direct, useful, and human without becoming cute.

## Anti-references

- Do not make the app feel like a generic AI dashboard with purple gradients, glass panels, oversized cards, or vague productivity copy.
- Do not expose internal machinery as the front door. Resolver, overlays, traces, and connector fabric are trust features, not primary navigation.
- Do not create new top-level tabs for every module. Related work should live together around the GTM loop.
- Do not hide the brain or make AI feel opaque. Users should be able to inspect what the system used.
- Do not imitate a social scheduler, CRM, ads manager, or generic AI writer. Tuezday sits above those tools as the shared memory and orchestration layer.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the GTM loop, not the build plan.
   Navigation should make the relationship between surfaces obvious: context lives in Brain, work gathers in Campaigns, output starts in Create, decisions happen in Review, contacts live in Audience, and configuration lives in Settings.

2. Make the brain visible but not heavy.
   The Brain is the moat. It should be easy to edit and inspect, but it should not force every user through resolver mechanics before they can act.

3. Use color as coded system state.
   The six brand hues represent meaning: belief, voice, history, ICP, system, and signals. In the product UI, color should help users understand source, channel, state, and priority.

4. Keep humans in control.
   Approval states, spend guardrails, publishing, and brain updates need clear decision surfaces. Nothing important should feel like it happened behind the user's back.

5. Dense, not cluttered.
   The app is an operations surface. It can carry real information density, but grouping, labels, hierarchy, and empty states must keep the task clear.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for text contrast and keyboard navigation. Body text must meet at least 4.5:1 contrast. Focus states must be visible. Interactive targets should be usable at desktop and mobile sizes. Motion should be restrained and must respect reduced-motion preferences. Color should never be the only indicator of state.
